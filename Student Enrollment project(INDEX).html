Limitations & Conclusion

  • Historical Depth: With only 5 years of data (2021–2025), the model cannot account for long-term 10-year academic cycles.
  • External Disruptions: The model is purely quantitative; it cannot predict "black swan" events such as sudden changes in government education policy or the introduction of a new competitor course.
  • Cold Start Sensitivity: For departments that recently hit zero (like Computer Animation), the model effectively "shuts down" the forecast. It cannot predict a department "re-opening" without manual intervention.

Conclusion: For small, highly fluctuating datasets like college enrollment, a Multi-Model Ensemble Approach is significantly superior to standard Machine Learning. By combining ARIMA and ETS, we created a system that is sensitive enough to catch new trends (like the Data Science boom) but stable enough to ignore random yearly noise, providing a balanced roadmap for administrative decision-making through 2028.

The use of Ensemble forecasting reduced our overall error rate by roughly 8% compared to a single ARIMA model.
